# 🧠 WebGuard v6: Detailed AI Model Architecture
*(Presentation Guide for Technical Review)*

The WebGuard v6 Threat Detection Engine is not a single algorithm, but a **Hybrid Meta-Ensemble**. It combines three distinct AI methodologies—Linear, Deep Learning, and Tree-Based—to evaluate URLs from multiple perspectives. 

Below is a detailed breakdown of the three core models trained in our system.

---

## 1. The Foundation Model: Support Vector Machine (SVM)
**Model Type:** Linear Classifier with TF-IDF Vectorization
**Role:** The fast, structural baseline.

* **How it works:** SVM operates by finding the optimal hyperplane (or line) that separates benign URLs from malicious ones in a high-dimensional space. Before feeding verbs to the SVM, we use **TF-IDF (Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency)**. This converts raw URL text into a massive 25,000-dimension numeric matrix representing the importance of specific words (like `login`, `secure`, `admin`, `paypal`).
* **Why we use it:** SVMs are incredibly fast and highly effective at identifying known "bad words" or specific structural patterns in a URL. If a URL contains explicit malicious keywords (e.g., `cmd.exe`, `wp-login.php`), the SVM will catch it almost instantly with high confidence.
* **Limitations:** It treats URLs as "bags of words" and struggles to understand the *sequence* or *context* of those words, making it vulnerable to obfuscated or highly scrambled zero-day URLs.
* **v6 Performance:** 95.02% Standalone Accuracy.

---

## 2. The Deep Learning Layer: CNN + BiLSTM
**Model Type:** Neural Network (1D Convolutional Neural Network + Bidirectional Long Short-Term Memory)
**Role:** The deep context and sequence analyzer.

* **How it works:** This is the heaviest and most advanced layer of the system. 
  1. **Embedding & Padding:** Every character in the URL is mapped to a dense vector (Embedding), and padded to a strict maximum length of 550 characters.
  2. **CNN (Spatial Pattern Recognition):** A 1D Convolutional Neural Network scans across the URL looking for local, spatial patterns of malicious behavior (e.g., weird clusters of symbols, hidden JavaScript tags).
  3. **BiLSTM (Contextual Sequence):** The data is then passed to a Bidirectional LSTM. Unlike the SVM, the BiLSTM reads the URL *forwards and backwards*, allowing it to understand the context and long-term dependencies of the URL structure (e.g., noticing that a `.php` file execution is happening at the very end of a deeply layered, obfuscated path).
* **Why we use it:** It excels at catching heavily obfuscated, zero-day, and complex phishing URLs that bypass standard keyword checks. It understands the "shape" of a malicious URL rather than just specific bad words.
* **v6 Performance:** ~98.03% Standalone Accuracy.

---

## 3. The Meta-Learner: XGBoost (Extreme Gradient Boosting)
**Model Type:** Ensemble Decision Tree
**Role:** The final decision maker (The "Judge").

* **How it works:** XGBoost doesn't look at the raw URL directly. Instead, it looks at the **predictions** generated by the SVM and the CNN, combined with **13 Handcrafted Numeric Features** (like URL length, number of subdomains, and mathematical *entropy*). It builds hundreds of decision trees sequentially, with each new tree attempting to correct the errors made by the previous trees.
* **Why we use it:** We use XGBoost to calculate the final "Malicious Probability." It acts as a tie-breaker. For example: If the SVM is 100% sure a URL is safe (because it lacks bad words), but the CNN is 80% sure it's dangerous (because the sequence looks extremely bizarre), the XGBoost model evaluates those probabilities alongside the URL's entropy and Brand Spoofing heuristic to make an incredibly accurate, weighted final decision.
* **The "Recall Penalty" Upgrade:** In v6, we applied a strict **2.5x sample weight** to the XGBoost training phase. This means we artificially "punished" the AI more severely if it accidentally classified a malicious URL as safe. This ensures the final Meta-Learner is highly paranoid and aggressive at catching threats (maximizing Recall).
* **v6 Performance:** 98.77% Master Accuracy across 446k URLs.

---

## ⚙️ How They Work Together (The Pipeline)
When a user clicks a link:
1. The URL is cleaned and normalized.
2. The **SVM** gives a rapid baseline prediction based on keywords.
3. The **CNN-BiLSTM** gives a deep-context prediction based on characters and sequence.
4. The system calculates 13 Handcrafted features (Entropy, Brand Spoofing flags, etc).
5. All of this data (SVM Prob + CNN Prob + 13 Features) is fed into **XGBoost**.
6. **XGBoost** outputs a final confidence score (0.0 to 1.0) which is compared against our dynamic bypass thresholds to either block or allow the page.
